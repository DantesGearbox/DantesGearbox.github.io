Water Shaders

Two different water shaders made with two different approaches.

The first shader: The Wave Equation, The Fresnel Effect

The first water shader is used in a scene with a small pool of water. The shader uses the Wave Equation, which is very good for small bodies of water such as the pool. The equation is simple and rather cost-effective since it only models waves and their height; not actual water flow. The height calculations can be seen in image 1. The method also takes into account the waves bouncing off the wall and the ressonance/dissonance that this creates.

The first water shader also uses the Fresnel effect to model how the water reflects the surroundings. This effect can be seen in image 2; the shaper the viewing angle the more the water reflects the surroundings.

The combined effect can be seen in Image 3

The second shader: Artistic and simple

While the first shader is more technically impressive, I tried making this shader look good on larger bodies of water while being simple and cost-effective. Because the shader has to be used on large areas it would be very costly to model the wave height like in the first shader.

Instead, a seamless heightmap is moved across the water quad and creates the wave-like effect. A corresponding, seamless normal map is moved along with the heightmap.
These normals are used in the calculation of specular highlights and a simplified Fresenel Effect that makes the water more reflective the sharper the viewing-angle is.
